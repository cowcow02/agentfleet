Theme Direction: 3 Approaches

Each approach shows a light theme + softened dark theme. Teal/green accent, warm off-white base, system preference + manual toggle.

A

Approach A: "Warm Canvas"

Light: Warm cream backgrounds (#FAF8F5), soft sand surfaces (#F2EDE7), earthy teal accent (#0D9488). Feels like a well-designed notebook app.

Dark: Warm charcoal (#1E1E24), medium-gray surfaces (#28282F). Lifted significantly from the current near-black. Cozy evening feel.

Vibe: Calm, organic, premium. Think Linear meets Notion with warmth.

B

Approach B: "Soft Studio"

Light: Subtle warm white (#FDFBF9), very light warm gray surfaces (#F5F1EC), vibrant teal (#14B8A6). Clean with just a hint of warmth.

Dark: Neutral dark gray (#222228), slightly warm surfaces (#2C2C33). Modern dark mode that doesn't strain eyes.

Vibe: Professional but friendly. Think Vercel meets a warm coffee shop. Slightly less warm than A.

C

Approach C: "Natural Light"

Light: Creamy off-white (#F8F5F0), warm beige surfaces (#EDE8E0), deep teal (#0F766E). Distinctly warm — you'd notice the warmth immediately.

Dark: Warm dark brown-gray (#1C1B1F), brown-tinted surfaces (#262529). Like a dimmed version of the light theme rather than a separate cold palette.

Vibe: Distinctive and characterful. Warmer than typical SaaS. Stands out from the crowd.

My recommendation: B (Soft Studio) — It strikes the best balance. Warm enough to feel inviting without being so warm that it feels unusual for a dev tool. The dark mode stays comfortable without being jarring. Easiest to get right across all UI states.
